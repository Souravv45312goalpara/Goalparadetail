THE IMPROVEMENT IN GOALPARA DISTRICT

Recent development projects in Goalpara district have focused on infrastructure, environment, healthcare, and skill development. The district's performance in the NITI Aayog's Aspirational Districts program shows significant improvement, particularly in health, agriculture, and water resources. Infrastructure and connectivity

National Highway projects: Several road development projects are in progress. These include the four-laning of the National Highway from Pancharatna to Dhupdhara and the two-laning of the Dudhnoi to Dainadubi road.

Industrial development: The government is establishing the Industrial Estate Rangjuli and the Growth Centre at Matia to boost industry and create jobs.

Public transport: In September 2025, the Assam State Transport Corporation (ASTC) issued a tender for repairing its station in Goalpara.

Urban development: In April 2025, the Asian Development Bank (ADB) prepared a draft Initial Environmental Examination for the augmentation of the water supply scheme in Goalpara town. The project also includes 10-year operation and maintenance.

Environmental initiatives

Afforestation: A massive "One Person, One Sapling" initiative led to the planting of over 12.6 lakh saplings. This increased the district's forest cover from 19% to 23%, helping to combat soil erosion and improve groundwater recharge.

Climate resilience: The Goalpara sub-project of the Climate Resilient Brahmaputra Integrated Flood and Riverbank Erosion Risk Management Project is underway to mitigate the effects of flooding.

Forest protection: In July 2025, the district began a major eviction drive to clear approximately 140 hectares of forest land from illegal encroachments, following a high court order. Health, education, and social welfare

Healthcare services: In April 2025, Goalpara secured the 5th position in the NITI Aayog's delta rankings for improved maternal and child health services. The district has also seen the opening of private clinics and therapy centers.

Vocational training: The Badhapur Van Dhan Vikash Kendra Cluster is being developed to empower the tribal community with sustainable livelihood options.

School education: In July 2025, the District Commissioner reviewed the progress of education department activities, including the construction of classrooms at several high schools under the Assam School Education and Adolescent Well-being Project.

Adult education: The Zila Lok Siksha Samiti was formed with the aim of spreading literacy among adults through the Saakshar Bharat Mission.

Tourism development

Promotion of sites: The government promotes existing attractions like the ancient Sri Surya Pahar, Dadan Hillock, and the Tukreswari Temple to attract visitors.

Adventure tourism: Opportunities for trekking and rock climbing in the Tukreswari and Rakhyashini hills are highlighted to draw adventure lovers.
