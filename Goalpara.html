WELLCOME TO GOALPARA

INTRODUCTION

Goalpara is an administrative district in the state of Assam, India, established in 1983 and situated on the south bank of the Brahmaputra River. The district, which is bordered by Meghalaya, Kamrup, and Dhubri districts, has a rich history dating back to the Koch dynasty. It is known for its archaeological sites, such as Sri Surya Pahar, which features ancient remains of Buddhist, Jain, and Hindu heritage, and the Tukreswari Temple, a significant Shakti Peetha.

HISTORY

Goalpara's history includes its rise to prominence under British rule, its annexation to Assam, and its modern division into sub-districts. The region's name may derive from "Gwaltippia," meaning "village of the milkmen". The original Goalpara district was established in 1822 and later separated from the Garo Hills district in 1866 before the current Goalpara district was formed in 1983. Ancient origins: The area has an ancient past, with archaeological remains found at sites like Sri Surya Pahar. British rule: The region came under British control in 1765. In 1822, the East India Company created the original Goalpara district, which included the area of the Bijni Kingdom. Separation and annexation: The Garo Hills district was separated from Goalpara in 1866, and Goalpara itself was annexed to Assam in 1874. Modern era: The present-day Goalpara district was formed in 1983 from the former Goalpara district. It is divided into two sub-divisions: Goalpara Sadar and North Salmara Civil.

FEMOUS PLACE

Goalpara's famous places include the archaeological site of Sri Surya Pahar, the large natural Urpad Beel lake, and religious sites like the Majar-Sharif of Pir Saheb and the Sri Sri Shyamrai Satra. Other notable spots are Dadan Hillock, Nandeswar Devalaya, and various waterfalls and scenic viewpoints like Chitimtim Dare and the Clear View Point.
